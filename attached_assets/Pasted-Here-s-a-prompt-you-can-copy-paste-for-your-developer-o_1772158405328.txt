Prompt for App Styling (Black & Gold Theme)

I’m happy with all existing functionality in the app. Do not change or remove any functionality, flows, logic, or navigation. Your job is only to update the visual styling.

Goal

Apply a new visual theme so the app matches a black + gold brand, consistent with a simple golden feather/pen logo.

1. Global Theme
	•	Background
	•	Set the main app background to pure or near-pure black (#000000 or very close).
	•	All main screens, menus, and modals should use a black background unless otherwise noted for contrast.
	•	Primary Color – Gold
	•	Use a rich gold color for all primary UI elements. Example: #FFD700 or #DAA520 (pick one and use it consistently).
	•	This gold should visually match a metallic golden pen/feather logo.
	•	Secondary/Accent Gold
	•	For subtle accents (borders, dividers, less important icons), you can use a slightly softer/darker gold variant (e.g., #B8860B) so there’s a clear hierarchy between primary and secondary elements.

2. Text & Typography
	•	Body Text
	•	Main text on black background: off-white or light gray (e.g., #F5F5F5 or #E0E0E0) for readability.
	•	Do not use pure gold for long paragraphs; reserve gold for emphasis.
	•	Headings / Important Labels
	•	Use gold for headings, section titles, key labels, and important numbers/metrics.
	•	Make sure font weight/size clearly differentiates headings from body text.
	•	Links, Highlights, and Emphasis
	•	Any clickable text links, highlighted values, or key status labels should use gold text.

3. Buttons & Interactive Elements
	•	Primary Buttons
	•	Background: gold
	•	Text: black or very dark gray for contrast.
	•	On hover/press: slightly darker gold or subtle glow effect.
	•	Secondary / Outline Buttons
	•	Transparent or black background.
	•	Gold border and gold text.
	•	On hover/press: light gold fill or stronger gold border.
	•	Icons
	•	All icons should be gold on black background wherever possible.
	•	If an icon is inside a gold button, the icon can be black/dark to maintain contrast.

4. Inputs, Cards, and Components
	•	Input Fields (text fields, dropdowns, etc.)
	•	Background: black or very dark gray.
	•	Border: thin gold outline (or gold on focus).
	•	Placeholder text: muted gray.
	•	Active/focused state: stronger gold border or subtle gold glow.
	•	Cards / Panels
	•	Background: very dark gray/black with gold borders or gold top lines.
	•	Titles in gold, content text in off-white.
	•	Dividers & Lines
	•	Use thin gold lines/dividers instead of gray.

5. States & Feedback
	•	Success, Error, Warning
	•	Keep existing logic and behavior exactly the same.
	•	You may:
	•	Keep standard colors (green/red/orange) but integrate small gold accents (icons, borders, or titles), or
	•	Use gold as the base with subtle colored icons.
	•	Make sure messages are still clearly distinguishable (success vs error).
	•	Hover/Active/Disabled
	•	Hover/active states should use variations of gold (brighter or darker).
	•	Disabled elements should be dimmed/desaturated but still aligned to the gold/black palette.

6. Logo Integration
	•	Place the golden feather/pen logo on a black background where appropriate (e.g., splash screen, top bar, or main header).
	•	Ensure its gold matches or is very close to the primary gold color used in the theme.

7. Constraints (Very Important)
	•	Do not change any existing business logic, data flows, or app functionalities.
	•	Do not remove or rearrange screens or features.
	•	This task is styling-only:
	•	Update colors, fonts, and spacing as needed.
	•	Adjust only CSS/theme variables, design tokens, or styling files.
	•	After changes, confirm:
	•	All buttons, links, and interactions still work exactly as before.
	•	No functional regression has been introduced.

Key caveats:
	•	Your developer may need to slightly adjust gold shades for readability/accessibility on different screens.
	•	If your app uses a design system (e.g., themes or tokens), they should map these instructions into that system rather than hard-coding colors in many places.
