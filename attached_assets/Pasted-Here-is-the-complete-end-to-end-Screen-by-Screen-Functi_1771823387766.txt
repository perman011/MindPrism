Here is the complete, end-to-end Screen-by-Screen Functional Requirements & UI/UX Blueprint.
This document acts as the exact translation of your business mission into a tangible app. It maps out the entire user journey from the moment they open the app, detailing the specific features on each page, the logic behind them, and exactly how they will look to the user on iOS, Android, and Web.
Page 1: The Welcome & Value Proposition (Splash Screen)
Purpose: To immediately hook the user and explain that they never have to read a 500-page self-help book again.
 * How it looks (UI/UX):
   * Vibe: A clean, full-screen, minimalist interface with calming, premium gradients (e.g., deep blues or soft sage greens).
   * Visuals: The center of the screen features a beautifully animated, auto-swiping carousel.
     * Slide 1: A heavy, dusty book magically shrinking down into a glowing brain. Text: "Skip the 500 pages. Master the principles."
     * Slide 2: Abstract UI showing charts and headphones. Text: "Visuals, Exercises, and Audio Summaries for the busy mind."
   * Buttons: Anchored at the very bottom are two large, pill-shaped buttons: A solid primary button ("Get Started") and a transparent text button ("I already have an account").
 * Functional Features (What it does):
   * Auto-Scrolling Logic: The carousel automatically advances every 3.5 seconds, but users can also manually swipe left/right.
   * Routing Logic: Detects if a user’s device already has an active login token; if yes, it bypasses this screen entirely and jumps to Page 4 (Home). Otherwise, the buttons route the user to Page 2.
Page 2: Authentication (The Sign-In Hub)
Purpose: To get the user into the app in under 5 seconds with zero friction.
 * How it looks (UI/UX):
   * Layout: The app logo sits at the top. The middle of the screen features a vertical stack of large, highly recognizable buttons with native brand icons.
   * Divider: A faint horizontal line with the word "OR" in the middle.
   * Inputs: Clean text boxes for "Email" and "Password" that glow slightly when tapped.
 * Functional Features (What it does):
   * Single Sign-On (SSO) APIs: Buttons for "Continue with Apple" (Mandatory for iOS approval), "Continue with Google", and "Continue with Facebook". Tapping these triggers the native phone OS prompt for 1-click biometric login (FaceID/Fingerprint).
   * Traditional Auth Engine: Standard email/password fields with a "Forgot Password?" routing link that triggers a secure password-reset email via the backend.
   * Compliance Gate: A mandatory, clickable checkbox for "I agree to the Terms of Service & Privacy Policy." The sign-in buttons remain grayed-out and unclickable until this is checked.
Page 3: Personalization Onboarding (The Mindset Map)
Purpose: To tailor the app’s algorithm to the user’s specific psychological goals. (Note: This appears only once, right after they create an account).
 * How it looks (UI/UX):
   * Header: A bold question at the top: "What areas of your mind do you want to master?" with a progress bar (Step 1 of 1).
   * Interactive Grid: The center is filled with floating, colorful "Chips" or "Tiles" featuring emojis. Examples: [🧠 Overcome Anxiety], [⏱️ Ultimate Productivity], [🤝 Read Body Language], [💼 Leadership].
   * Bottom Action: A sticky "Build My Library" button that floats over the grid.
 * Functional Features (What it does):
   * Multi-Select Engine: Users can tap multiple tiles. When tapped, the tile triggers a subtle haptic vibration (phone buzzes), changes to a vibrant color, and displays a checkmark.
   * Database Write: When the user hits "Build My Library", the app saves these specific goal-tags to their backend user profile database.
   * Algorithm Trigger: The app instantly uses these tags to curate and populate the content they will see on the next page.
Page 4: The Home Dashboard (The Main Feed)
Purpose: The central daily hub. This is where users land every time they open the app to discover content and track their habits.
 * How it looks (UI/UX):
   * Top Bar: A friendly greeting ("Good Morning, [Name]") and a "Flame" icon showing their consecutive daily streak (e.g., 🔥 3).
   * Hero Card ("The Daily Spark"): A large, beautiful, shareable graphic card taking up the top third of the screen. It features one profound psychological principle or quote for the day.
   * Netflix-Style Rows: Below the hero card are horizontally scrollable carousels of book covers. Headers read: "Based on Your Goals", "Trending Audio Summaries", and "Jump Back In".
   * Global Bottom Nav: 4 fixed icons at the very bottom: [🏠 Home], [🔍 Discover], [🎧 Audio], [👤 My Vault].
 * Functional Features (What it does):
   * Resume State Tracker: The "Jump Back In" row remembers exactly where the user left off in a summary (e.g., showing a progress ring at 45% on Atomic Habits) and drops them right back on the exact principle or audio timestamp.
   * Streak Calculator: If the user spends at least 3 minutes interacting with content, the backend adds a day to their "🔥 Streak" to gamify the experience.
   * Content Randomizer: The "Daily Spark" widget automatically fetches a new, randomized quote/principle from the database every 24 hours at midnight.
Page 5: Discover & Search
Purpose: For users who want to manually browse the entire catalog of extracted psychology books.
 * How it looks (UI/UX):
   * Top: A prominent search bar with a magnifying glass icon.
   * Category Pills: A horizontal, swipeable row of selectable category buttons (e.g., CBT, Stoicism, Habit Building, Neurodivergence).
   * Grid View: The rest of the page is a visually appealing 2-column grid of "Summary Cards" featuring modern, custom cover art.
 * Functional Features (What it does):
   * Predictive Search API: As the user types "anx...", the app instantly suggests books, authors, or specific principles related to anxiety before they even hit enter.
   * Dynamic Filtering: Tapping a Category Pill instantly re-sorts the grid view below to show only books tagged with that category.
Page 6: The Summary Lobby (Book Detail Page)
Purpose: Sets expectations before the user dives into the content, showing them exactly how much time they are saving compared to reading the 500-page book.
 * How it looks (UI/UX):
   * Hero Image: High-quality cover art of the original book sitting at the top, fading seamlessly into the background color.
   * Metrics: Quick-glance badges: "🎧 15 Min Audio" | "🧠 5 Principles" | "📊 3 Visuals" | "✍️ 2 Exercises"
   * Dual-Action Buttons: Two massive, side-by-side buttons: [✨ Start Interactive Journey] (Solid Color) and [▶️ Play Audio] (Outlined).
   * Syllabus Accordion: A vertical list showing the modules inside (e.g., 🔽 Principle 1: The Dopamine Loop).
 * Functional Features (What it does):
   * Offline Download Manager: A cloud icon in the top right. Tapping it triggers a background download of all text, interactive JSON, and MP3 audio files for this specific book, saving it to local storage for airplane/commute mode.
   * Dual-Routing Logic: Routes the user to completely different user interfaces depending on if they want to Read/Interact (Page 7) or Listen (Page 9).
Page 7: The "Interactive Engine" (The Core App USP)
Purpose: This entirely replaces the 500-page book. It delivers the extracted principles, stories, and visual frameworks without overwhelming walls of text.
 * How it looks (UI/UX):
   * Story-Style UI: Operates heavily like Instagram Stories, Snapchat, or Duolingo. The content fills the whole screen.
   * Progress Bar: A segmented bar runs across the top showing how many "Cards" are left in this summary.
   * Text/Story Cards: Clean, heavy typography on a solid background stating a core psychological rule, or a short 3-sentence extracted anecdote backing up the rule.
   * Visual Cards: Rich, vibrant infographics. Example: A blank pyramid representing Maslow's Hierarchy.
 * Functional Features (What it does):
   * Swipe/Tap Navigation: Invisible tap zones. Tapping the right edge of the screen smoothly slides the next card in. Tapping left goes back.
   * Interactive "Tap-to-Reveal" Logic: On Visual Cards, the user must physically interact with the screen. Tapping the blank tiers of the pyramid triggers a smooth micro-animation that reveals the text explanation for that tier.
   * Highlight & Save: Users can long-press any text or visual on the screen to highlight it and send it instantly to their private Vault.
Page 8: The Action Engine (Interactive Exercises)
Purpose: Fulfills your mission of making users apply the psychology to their own lives. (These usually appear as the final "Cards" in the Page 7 journey).
 * How it looks (UI/UX):
   * Looks like a sleek, digital workbook embedded directly into the app.
   * Displays a prompt extracted from the book: (e.g., "Identify your biggest trigger for procrastination today.")
   * Underneath is a large, expansive text box.
   * A prominent "Save to Journal" button at the bottom.
 * Functional Features (What it does):
   * Dynamic Inputs: Auto-expanding text boxes that bring up the device's native keyboard. It also supports interactive sliding scales (e.g., drag a slider from red to green to rate stress levels 1-10).
   * Auto-Save & Encryption: As the user types personal thoughts, the app auto-saves locally every 3 seconds. When they hit "Save", the text is encrypted for privacy and pushed to their secure database profile.
   * Gamified Reward: Upon clicking "Save", a celebratory animation (digital confetti or a satisfying checkmark) plays on screen to trigger a dopamine response and reinforce the habit.
Page 9: The Global Audio Player (Audiobook Summaries)
Purpose: For the busy professional who wants to consume the audiobook summaries hands-free (driving, gym, walking).
 * How it looks (UI/UX):
   * Full Screen Mode: Heavily inspired by Spotify or Apple Podcasts. Large, blurred album art in the background. Crisp book cover art in the center. A standard, intuitive timeline scrubber to drag through the audio. Massive, easy-to-tap playback controls (Play, Pause, Rewind 15s, Fast Forward 15s).
   * Mini-Player Mode: If the user swipes down, the player shrinks to a floating "mini-player" bar at the bottom of the screen so they can keep browsing the app while listening.
 * Functional Features (What it does):
   * Background Audio API: Crucial feature. Integrates with iOS Control Center, Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, and lock screens. Audio continues playing seamlessly when the phone is locked or the user opens another app.
   * Playback Speed Controller: A toggle to cycle the MP3 speed through 1.0x, 1.25x, 1.5x, and 2.0x without distorting the narrator's pitch.
   * "View Visual" Sync Button: A unique button that allows the user to instantly pull up the interactive infographic (from Page 7) that the narrator is currently talking about in the audio file.
Page 10: My Growth Vault (User Profile & Saved Data)
Purpose: A private, centralized repository where users can review the ROI of the app by seeing their progress, revisited exercises, and saved diagrams.
 * How it looks (UI/UX):
   * Top Section (Stats): Three modern metric squares: "🔥 12 Day Streak", "🎧 450 Mins Listened", "📝 12 Exercises Done".
   * Tabbed Navigation: A horizontal menu to switch between 3 folders:
     * 📁 My Journal: A beautiful vertical timeline showing all the typed exercises from Page 8. Each entry shows the date, the book it came from, the prompt, and the user's saved answer.
     * 📁 Saved Visuals: A grid gallery of the interactive diagrams the user bookmarked.
     * 📁 Downloads: A list of audio summaries saved to the device for offline mode.
   * Settings Gear: A standard gear icon in the top right corner.
 * Functional Features (What it does):
   * Database Querying: Automatically fetches and organizes all the specific JSON and text data tied to the user's unique ID.
   * Export Data Function: A button allowing users to export their Journal Entries as a nicely formatted PDF to their phone files or email.
   * Subscription & Account Management: Accessed via the gear icon. Connects to backend billing (Apple In-App Purchases, Google Play Billing, Stripe) for users to upgrade to the Premium Tier, toggle Dark/Light mode, and manage push notifications.
