To explain the interactivity you're seeing in that Instagram Reel—which features Ryan Sihag showcasing a modern WordPress design—you should focus on three core concepts: Micro-interactions, State Transitions, and Fluid Motion.
When you describe this to your developers or designers, use these terms to capture that "dynamic" feel:
1. Micro-interactions
These are the small, functional animations that respond to a user's specific action (like a hover or a click). In the UI shown, notice how elements don't just "appear"—they react.
 * Hover States: The way cards or buttons might subtly lift, glow, or expand when the cursor moves over them.
 * Feedback Loops: Instant visual confirmation, such as a heart popping up or a button changing color, which makes the app feel responsive and alive.
2. Layout Transitions & "Smart" Grids
The video likely highlights a fluid composition where elements rearrange themselves smoothly.
 * Non-Linear Layouts: Moving away from rigid boxes to organic shapes or overlapping elements.
 * Staggered Reveals: Instead of the whole page loading at once, elements "cascade" in one after another, creating a sense of narrative flow.
3. Scroll-Triggered Animations
A major part of that "Instagram-style" polish is how the UI responds as you scroll.
 * Parallax Effects: Background layers moving slower than foreground layers to create depth.
 * Reveal on Scroll: Elements fading or sliding into view only when the user reaches that section, which keeps the interface clean and focused.
